Seating Tips

Where you sit in a theatre has a huge impact on the overall experience of the performance. While most of the time you will be able to hear everything no matter your seat location, different seat locations offer different viewing experiences and some are objectively superior to others. The following information aims to help you decide where the best location is for you!

Orchestra

The Orchestra is the first tier of seating, usually consisting of all of the seats on the ground. These seats are generally the best and most expensive.

Tier Description Price Level
Center Orchestra These seats are the best of the best. Located in the middle of the theater on the lowest level, these seats usually provide a great, centered, up-close view of the performers and their costumes. $$$$$
Left/Right Orchestra These seats are generally very good. Located on the sides of the Center Orchestra on the lowest level of the theater, these seats still provide a great, up-close view of the performance, though some details can get hidden or missed due to the viewing angle, especially if located on the extreme right or left of this section. $$$$
Rear Orchestra These seats are usually located underneath the overhang of the Mezzanine section. As such, they can occasionally result in a partial view of some stage effects. These seats are the farthest away from the stage on the lower level of the theatre, and are usually considered worse than premium Mezzanine seats. $$$

Mezzanine

The Mezzanine is a raised level that usually extends over the back third of the Orchestra. The first several rows of the Mezzanine are often of similar quality and price to Orchestra and offer a higher view of the production.

Tier Description Price Level
Center Mezzanine These are fantastic seats in the center of the first raised level in the theatre. The first several rows of this section are often the same horizontal distance from the stage as many Center Orchestra seats. These seats offer a great view of the whole production and give a solid view of the overall production. These seats are generally still close enough to see all facial expressions and choreography. Many prefer the first rows of the Mezzanine over Orchestra seats. $$$$
Left/Right Mezzanine These seats are located to the sides of the Center Mezzanine and usually give good views of the overall production. Some sightlines may not be the best, though facial details and costuming can usually still be seen well from these seats. $$$
Rear Mezzanine These seats are located in the back of the first raised level. If the theatre has a balcony, these seats are generally located beneath the balcony overhang, which can lead to a partial view of some stage effects. These seats may be considered worse than the best balcony seats. $$

Balcony

Some theatres have another raised level above the Mezzanine level. Not all theaters have this section, and it is not commonly found in most Broadway theatres. These seats are usually the highest and farthest away from the stage; thus they are generally the lowest quality viewing experience and lowest price.

Tier Description Price Level
Front Balcony These seats are in the front of the second raised level. They are often quite high and far away from the stage, but can still provide a good view of the overall production. Many facial and costume details will be difficult to see at this range. $$
Rear Balcony These seats are the very highest and farthest seats from the stage in the theatre. Stage details will be difficult to clearly make out at this range, though sound is usually still stellar. These are generally the most affordable tickets in the theatre. Stage effects that take place on raised platforms, catwalks, or aerial choreography may be hidden at this level. $
